COHABITAT RENOVATION

Living in a cohabitat brings many additional aspects to consider as an architect. This project was about the integration of a cohabitat into a housing project in Leuven. It was important to respect the prevailing dynamics within the different levels. On the scale of the whole complex, within a building but also within a single apartment. I decided not to make tabula rasa with the existing building but rather to analyse how individual architectural elements can change an originally separated apartment into a functioning cohabitat. The main requirements were therefore not to demolish anything unnecessarily and furthermore to give the project a common passion. With a theatre and a ﬁlm room, the project has two hearts with different degrees of intimacy and can thus activate and animate the residential environment.

SPORT + HABITAT

As part of a general district project, the land adjacent to a former industrial site was developed and renovated. For the selected site, apartments and a sports hall were integrated into a joint project. The challenges here were to understand the urbanistic background of the changing district. It was also important to respect and work with the different borders, such as the limit between nature and city or the balance between public and private, which is reﬂected in the program itself.

INTER SPECIES SHELTER

The following project was born from the uniﬁcation of two completely independent intentions: To develop a solution to the increasing homelessness rates and to allow the best possible integration into the ﬂoral and faunal environment. Based on the use of abandoned railway containers, this project offers a cheap and simple way of construction. After an intensive analysis of the context, beneﬁcial interactions with the ﬂora and fauna has been developed.

RESPONSIVE FACADE

Within the framework of the digital fabrication studio I have concentrated on the experimentation and evaluation of kinetic facades. I started with an analysis of the existing mechanics. The next step was to develop several different prototypes in order to respect some of the advantages and disadvantages and to provide suggestions for improvement. After the experimental phase, the simulation was used to evaluate the advantages regarding the energy balance and the visible light irradiation.

ATELIER EXTENSION

The extension of the Lazzaretto Veccio in Venice was our ﬁrst major project within the framework of our studies. It was about the conversion of a fallow land into a studio for renovation students. The challenges were to work with the culture of the Lazzaretto directly beside and also to integrate it into the Venezia skylign without falling into a false old. This project continues the shell of the old building and thus enables an integration into the surrounding area. Furthermore, a larger part of the fallow land will be left untouched and made inaccessible, thus counteracting the uprooting of the site by this project.
